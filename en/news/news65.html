How To Install Acoustic Ceiling Tile

2013-04-18

The soft texture of acoustic ceiling tiles is designed to dampen noise, whether in public or private buildings. The suspended grid system in which the tiles are hung enables easy access for repairs and renovations to the hidden wires and pipes. Acoustic ceiling tiles are available in a range of sizes, colors and patterns, made to mimic traditional building materials or provide an interesting and beautiful design on your ceiling.

Instructions

1.Measure your ceiling carefully and draw a diagram on the graph paper. If your ceiling is slightly off square -- one end is wider than the other -- include that in your diagram.

2.Mark the center of one edge and the edge opposite it on the diagram. Draw a line between these two marks using the straightedge. Repeat with the other two edges.

3.Use the graph paper to draw a diagram of a tile arrangement. Place the center of a row of tiles along the center lines. Use this diagram to determine the number of wall angles, main runners, cross pieces and tiles you will need for your ceiling.

4.Mark a line on the wall with the chalk line at the manufacturer's recommended height for your acoustic ceiling tiles. Use the level to make sure the chalk line is straight and parallel to the floor.

5.Position the L-shaped wall angles with their top edges lined up with the chalk line and the bottom of the L sticking out from the wall. Nail the L-shaped wall angles to the walls, placing the nails 2 feet apart or less.

6.Stretch the string across the center of the room in either direction, forming a cross with the center point at the center point of the room.

7.Install the main runners by setting the tabs on either end on the wall angle at either end of the ceiling. Arrange them parallel to each other, making sure that they are as far apart as the ceiling tiles are wide. Make sure the notches in the main runners line up with each other, using the laser level.

8.Screw the eye hooks into the ceiling directly above each of the main runners at 4-foot intervals.

9.Wire the main runners to the ceiling. Run a length of wire through the holes in the main runners, up to the ceiling, and into the eye hooks.

10.Snap the cross pieces into the notches of the main runners to complete the grid.

11.Insert the acoustic tiles into the grid.

12.Cut edge tiles down to size by measuring the size of the available space. Add 3/8 inch. Cut a ceiling tile to this width, using the utility knife. Make a cut halfway through the thickness, 3/8 inch away from the newly cut edge. Slice through the cut edge of the tile, halfway through its thickness, to remove a strip and create a new flange. Insert the cut tile into its spot.

13.Cut holes for pipes, wires and vents with the utility knife where necessary.
